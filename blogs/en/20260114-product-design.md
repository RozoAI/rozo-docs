# Product Design
## From "Individual Sovereignty" to "Usable Individual Sovereignty"
### How We Use Intent-based Earn & Intent-based Pay to Guide Users Step by Step Into the Future

*January 14, 2026*

> This is not a "feature list."
> This is our roadmap for bringing **individual sovereignty** into the real world:
> **Start with usability → Start with not making mistakes → Start with daily life → Then gradually upgrade to stronger forms of sovereignty.**

---

## 1. The Core Problem We Solve: Sovereignty Must Be Usable

We believe the world is entering an era of "individual sovereignty."

But if sovereignty remains just a technical concept, it will stay in the hands of a few:
- Self-custody is too hard
- Operations are too complex
- The cost of mistakes is too high
- It can't be used in daily life

So sovereignty becomes "looks free, but actually hard to use."

Our judgment is very clear:

> **Sovereignty is not declared — it is continuously used.**
> **Scaling sovereignty must start with "convenient, safe, and daily."**

---

## 2. Core Method: Intent-based, Not Technology-driven

We don't make users learn the system. We let users express intent.

What users really want usually comes down to three things:
- I want to store my money well (not idle, preferably earning)
- I want to spend money (payments should be as natural as breathing)
- I want to make fewer mistakes (I don't want technical details to ruin everything)

Therefore, we adopt an Intent-based product approach:

> Users only need to say "what I want,"
> The system handles "how to do it best."

The system keeps complexity internal:
- Users don't need to understand chains, gas, bridges, protocol differences
- Users don't need to become engineers
- Users don't need to sacrifice life experience for sovereignty

---

## 3. Product Components (Phase One): Intent-based Earn + Intent-based Pay

We start with the two most real, most frequent things:
**Saving money (Earn)** and **Spending money (Pay)**.

These two things are the entry point for sovereignty into life.

---

### 3.1 Intent-based Earn: You Just Need to Say "I Want to Save and Earn Interest"

In a world of continuous inflation, "leaving money idle" is itself a loss.
Therefore, the primary need for sovereign assets is very simple:

> **My money shouldn't just sit there doing nothing.**

But reality is:
- Too many yield strategies
- Too hard to judge risk
- Too complex to operate
- So most people would rather do nothing

The principle of Intent-based Earn:

- **You just state intent:** "I want to save this money and generate yield."
- **System executes:** Automatically selects the more suitable path and strategy under current conditions
- **User still self-custodies:** Asset ownership always belongs to the user
- **Understandable, predictable:** Keep yield and risk within sustainable ranges

And we respect the most important point of sovereignty:

> **You can choose not to deposit.**
> This is your right.
> The choice itself is sovereignty.

---

### 3.2 Intent-based Pay: You Just Say "I Want to Pay," the Rest Is Handled by the System

Payment is a key step for sovereignty to enter life.

If a system can only "save money" but not "spend money,"
then it can only stay at the asset layer, unable to become life-layer infrastructure.

The principle of Intent-based Pay:

- **You just state intent:** "I want to pay X amount to Y"
- **System executes:** Handles routing, settlement, network differences, complex steps in the backend
- **Experience must be minimal:** As natural as Apple Pay
- **Still maintain self-custody:** Users don't need to hand over assets for convenience

We believe in a very strong judgment:

> **Truly strong sovereignty is not about making users understand more,
> but letting users have control even without understanding.**

---

## 4. User Incentives (Optional but Powerful): Cashback Brings Sovereignty Into Life Faster

When a system is usable enough, the next step is to make it used faster.

Cashback is not a "gimmick," but a growth strategy:
- Help users form daily usage habits
- Return value directly to users, not intermediate networks
- Make "lower cost payments" a perceptible advantage at the experience layer

But we also make clear:

> Cashback can be skipped.
> But if we do it, we must do it right —
> **Legal, compliant, sustainable.**

---

## 5. Compliance and Legal: We'd Rather Go Slower to Go Further

We understand a reality:
As soon as a product enters daily payments and reward systems, it touches regulatory and legal boundaries.

Therefore our attitude is:
- No edge cases
- No gray-area growth
- No using "decentralization" as an excuse to avoid responsibility

What we want to do is:

> **Under a better legal structure, make sovereignty "something ordinary people dare to use."**

Because if users don't dare to use it,
there is no real sovereignty adoption.

---

## 6. Future Path: Start with "Convenient," Gradually Upgrade to Stronger Sovereignty Forms

We never believe in the myth of "getting there in one step."

Truly successful sovereignty migration must be gradual:
1. **Convenient and easy to use** (users can get started, can use daily)
2. **Safe and hard to make mistakes** (structural security, recoverable failures)
3. **Sustainable asset behavior** (Earn / Pay becomes the daily default)
4. **Gradually enhance sovereignty attributes** (more chain support, BTC payments, stronger anti-freeze forms)

For example:
- Bitcoin payments
- Multi-chain asset support
- Stronger anti-freeze, anti-single-point-dependency capabilities

These are all on our upgrade roadmap.

But the key is:

> **Users don't need to be forced to upgrade.**
> **They can choose how far to upgrade at their own pace.**

This is what "individual sovereignty" truly mature looks like.

---

## 7. We're Not Asking Users to Be Independent Immediately
## We're Giving Them a Path That Won't Fail

Our mission is not "to make everyone leave the old world immediately,"
but to let users gradually gain more control in real life:

- Not hijacked by technology
- Not deterred by complexity
- Not zeroed out by one mistake
- Can save, can spend, can migrate
- And eventually move toward higher-intensity personal sovereignty

> **Independence is not the starting point.**
> **Independence is the result.**
> And what we need to do is build this path.
